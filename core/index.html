Core Principles

  • Empirical Spirituality: Base teachings on observable truths and promote critical thinking while incorporating scientific understanding and wonder.
  • Human-Centered Ethics: Focus on improving human well-being, reducing suffering, and fostering kindness, fairness, and justice.
  • Universal Inclusivity: Avoid exclusive claims of truth or superiority, celebrating diversity and shared humanity.
  • Adaptability: Allow teachings and practices to evolve based on new knowledge and collective needs.
  • Environmental Stewardship: Treat the natural world as sacred, emphasizing sustainability and ecological responsibility.

Rituals and Practices

  • Mindfulness and Reflection: Incorporate meditation, journaling, or quiet reflection to foster self-awareness and mental health.
  • Celebration of Life Stages: Mark milestones such as births, partnerships, and deaths with meaningful ceremonies.
  • Community Service: Encourage collective action to address global and local challenges.
  • Seasonal and Planetary Rituals: Celebrate natural phenomena to connect with the rhythms of the Earth.

Organizational Structure

  • Decentralized Leadership: Avoid hierarchical power structures, promoting leadership by example and consensus.
  • Transparency and Accountability: Ensure decisions and actions are open and subject to review.
  • Grassroots Focus: Empower local groups to adapt practices to their cultural contexts.

Symbols and Community Identity

  • Universal Symbols: Use inclusive symbols like a tree (growth and interconnection) or a circle (unity and eternity).
  • Personal Interpretation: Encourage individuals to find their own meaning in symbols and rituals.

Core Beliefs

  • Interconnectedness: Emphasize the interdependence of all life and the universe.
  • Personal Growth: Focus on self-improvement, learning, and becoming the best version of oneself.
  • Ethical Progress: Commit to creating a better world through collective effort and learning.

Avoiding Pitfalls

  • No Dogma or Absolutism: Encourage questioning and reinterpretation of teachings.
  • Resist Weaponization: Actively guard against using beliefs to justify harm or exclusion.
  • Reject Profit Motives: Ensure accessibility by preventing monetization of spiritual practices.
